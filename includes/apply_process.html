Apply to become a Teacher

The process is simple:

1. Check entry requirements.

2. Log on to UCAS teacher training or the DfE's Apply System: Apply for teacher training

3. Use the DfE search tool to find all our courses.

  • 4. Complete the application form ensuring:
      • your degree qualification is from a UK higher education institution or equivalent qualification comprising of 300 HE credit points, 60 of which are at level 6 of QCF (foundation degrees must be supplemented with 60 credits at level 6) (obtain NARIC confirmation for overseas qualifications)
      • you have achieved a standard equivalent to a grade C in the GCSE examinations in English and Mathematics.
      • For Primary a grade C in GCSE Science is also required.
  • 5. We will call you and arrange for an interview. You will be asked to:
      • complete a subject task
      • carry out a prioritising exercise
      • complete a professional task
      • present a short microteach session of approximately 5 minutesâ€™ duration
      • attend an interview with one of the SCITT coordinators and a subject teacher
  • 6. At interview, we will assess the following:
      • The depth of your subject knowledge
      • Your potential to become a teacher
      • Your understanding of teaching and current educational issues
  • 7. What you will need to bring to interview:
      • Original certificates for the qualifications stated on your application form
      • Proof of identity including: a passport, birth certificate, driving license and a utilities bill
  • 8. When will I hear if I have got a provisional place?
    • You will be informed of the decision including any conditions for an offer e.g. subject knowledge enhancement.
      We aim to inform you of a decision within 48 hours of your interview.
  • 9. Confirming my placement
    • You will need to log back on UCAS to confirm your intention to take the place.

The Solent SCITT and all partnership organisations are committed to safeguarding and promoting good welfare of pupils. We expect all trainees to share this commitment. All successful candidates will be subject to enhanced Disclosure and Barring Service checks to review suitability of working with pupils.
